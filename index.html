Our School Programme

We use the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC), which was introduced in 2017 to replace the 8-4-4 system. The CBC is designed to be more practical and learner-centered, with a focus on developing a student's competencies and skills, rather than just acquiring knowledge.

Overall, the CBC education system in Kenya is intended to equip learners with the skills, knowledge, and values needed to succeed in the 21st century.

The CBC approach is built on four key pillars: Competence-based, Holistic development, Inclusivity, and Relevance. The CBC aims to develop learners who can apply their skills and knowledge in real-life situations and can adapt to the changing demands of society. It also seeks to develop learners who are emotionally, socially, and physically healthy, and who can contribute positively to their communities and the country as a whole.

Digital Literacy

We focus on developing learners' ability to use digital technologies effectively and ethically, for communication, research, and problem-solving.

Self-efficacy

We develop learners' confidence, resilience, and self-motivation, and help them to become lifelong learners who are able to set and achieve goals, and adapt to changing circumstances.

Critical Thinking and Problem Solving

We develop learners' ability to analyze, evaluate, and solve complex problems, using a range of thinking skills and strategies.

Citizenship

We develop learners' understanding of their rights and responsibilities as citizens, and to encourage them to participate actively in their communities and society as a whole.

Communication

We focus on developing learners' ability to communicate effectively and confidently in a variety of contexts, including reading, writing, speaking, and listening.

Creativity and Imagination

This competency encourages learners to think creatively and to use their imagination to generate new ideas, perspectives, and solutions.
